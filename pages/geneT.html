Gene Technology

Humble Beginnings

Gene technology is the use of biotechnology to change an organism's genes. Since the discovery of DNA in the 1950s, progress on new discoveries has only increased. The original Human Genome Project, which was aimed to find and map all the genes in a human, took 13 years to complete and cost $3 billion. This same process can be performed today in just 3 days and costs less than $1000 (Royal Society 2017). Basic genetic technology already exists today in the form of genetic testing. It can be used to diagnose rare conditions or see what genes someone has inherited. Genetic testing detects changes (called mutations) in a person's genes to determine the likelihood of developing a genetic condition, like cancer.

However, advanced gene-editing technology is more emerging and subject to many ongoing ethical debates. One such example is CRISPR Cas9, which is the most efficient, fast and cheapest method currently available (MedlinePlus 2020).
